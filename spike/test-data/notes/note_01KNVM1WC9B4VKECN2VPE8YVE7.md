Most AI is a feature, not a product (2024 observation). This maps cleanly onto the AI startup prisoner's dilemma: if AI capability is an API call away, then any AI-powered 'product' is just a feature of the platform that owns the model. The AI browser example makes this concrete — these companies built distribution wrappers, not AI. They have no moat because they neither own the browser engine nor the AI. This is not a product trap specific to AI; it's the eternal trap of building on top of platforms you don't control. What's new is how fast the platform absorbed the differentiator.
