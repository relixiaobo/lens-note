Framing quality as professionalism is a rhetorical trap for developers. When developers argue for internal quality on the grounds of 'doing it right' or craftsmanship, they implicitly concede that quality is a cost — a moral expenditure. This concession loses the economic argument before it starts. The correct framing is purely financial: internal quality reduces future feature cost. Moralistic framing ironically entrenches the false trade-off it's trying to fight.
