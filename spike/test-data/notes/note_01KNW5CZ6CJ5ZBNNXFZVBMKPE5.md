The 'knowledge network' framing explains *why* simple explanation signals deep understanding. If knowledge is organized as a network, then being able to explain something simply means you've found the shortest path between that concept and the listener's existing network — you've mapped the new node to familiar ones. The simpler the explanation, the more efficiently the concept plugs into the existing structure. This connects the 'explain simply' heuristic to a structural account of comprehension, not just an empirical test.
