The Bitter Lesson implies that the most dangerous form of progress is progress that forecloses better approaches. Domain-knowledge injection doesn't just plateau — it 'complicates methods in ways that make them less suited to taking advantage of general methods.' This is a stronger claim than 'short-term gains don't last' — it's that certain short-term gains actively prevent the better long-term path. This maps precisely to how cruft in software doesn't just slow things down, it makes certain refactors structurally impossible.
