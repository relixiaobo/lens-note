The AirPods Pro 3 design process — 10,000+ 3D ear scans, 100,000+ hours of user research across Human Factors, Biomechanics, Acoustics, and Industrial Design — is a concrete instance of a rarely articulated principle: physical products serving diverse human bodies require empirical population-scale data, not idealized archetypes. This is the hardware analog to accessibility-by-design. The number is striking: 10,000 ear scans means the design space is defined by actual anatomical variance in the population, not a 'standard ear.' Most consumer hardware ships to a single form factor and calls it universal. The research scale here suggests Apple treats anatomical diversity as a first-class design constraint rather than an edge case.
