The productivity crossover happens within weeks, not months or years. A common managerial intuition is that cutting quality corners buys significant runway — enough to ship, validate, pivot. But Fowler's canvassing of experienced developers suggests the high-quality track overtakes the low-quality track after just a few weeks. This drastically narrows the window where 'move fast, fix later' is economically rational — arguably to only the shortest of throwaway prototypes.
