There is an open question that SQUIRE implicitly raises but does not resolve: where is the right boundary between what a structured IR can express and what requires free-form natural language? SquireIR's 'language support for controlled exploration and refinement' presumably has an expressiveness ceiling — things it cannot represent. When users hit that ceiling, they fall back to chat or re-prompting, recreating the original problem. The history of domain-specific languages suggests that every structured language creates a class of tasks it handles well and a class it handles poorly. The interesting design question for AI-assisted authoring tools is not 'structured vs. unstructured' but 'how do you gracefully degrade from structure to freeform when the user's intent exceeds the IR's expressiveness?'
