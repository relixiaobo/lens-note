Ng's multi-agent collaboration pattern includes *agent debate* as one of its forms: different agents argue competing positions to improve the final answer. This is a specific variant that the KB has not captured explicitly. The KB's notes on multi-agent parallelization distinguish between voting (same task, aggregate judgment) and splitting (different subtasks). Debate is a third form: *adversarial collaboration*, where agents are intentionally assigned opposing positions and their disagreement is the productive mechanism. This maps onto a known epistemic principle: adversarial collaboration (the 'red team / blue team' structure) surfaces assumptions that cooperative reasoning hides. The KB's note on 'multi-perspectival epistemology' (note_01KNWDPAKWKSNVENDSTG634K4Z) captures the deep structural reason — multiple independent contexts can access truths no single context can reach — but doesn't name debate as its most potent implementation.
