Arc II's bet on need-prediction as its differentiator is structurally the same as the longitudinal behavioral context argument (note_01KNW9VZ9K7JV80QKFRQ00HP6Z): competitive advantage in AI browsing accrues not to whoever calls the best model, but to whoever accumulates the richest behavioral model of the user. If predicting browsing needs requires understanding browsing history, intent patterns, and contextual signals, then the moat is in the data flywheel, not the AI layer. This supports and extends note_01KNVM1WC9B4VKECN2VPE8YVE7's thesis that AI browsers without model ownership are wrappers — Arc II implicitly acknowledges this by betting on behavioral prediction, which requires owning the user relationship deeply, as an alternative moat.
