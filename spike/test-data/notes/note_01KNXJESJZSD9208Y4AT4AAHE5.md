LaCy implicitly makes a structural argument *against* the Bitter Lesson's 'don't inject human knowledge' directive: it deliberately injects grammatical/syntactic structure (via spaCy) to improve the training signal. But this is not a counter-example — it's a refinement of where the lesson applies. The Bitter Lesson's ceiling is about injecting domain *knowledge* into the content of what a model learns. LaCy injects knowledge about *how to evaluate learning quality* — a meta-level injection. This suggests the Bitter Lesson should be applied more narrowly: the ceiling is on injected object-level knowledge, not on injected epistemological scaffolding that guides learning.
