The indirect vs. direct AI monetization distinction maps precisely onto Fowler's internal/external quality distinction: indirect monetization captures value users cannot directly perceive (retention uplift, conversion improvement), while direct monetization only works when users can consciously perceive and attribute the AI's value. Just as internal quality cannot be priced by users who cannot see it, AI features that improve metrics invisibly cannot be directly charged for — they must be monetized through the product they invisibly improve.
