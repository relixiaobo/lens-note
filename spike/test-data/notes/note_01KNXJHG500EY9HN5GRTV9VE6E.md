The strongest result in this paper may be the dialect clustering one: embedding-derived hierarchies match those derived from human listening experiments. This validates that the embedding space is not just computationally useful but that it encodes perceptual reality as humans experience it. This raises a deeper question: when learned representations align with human perceptual categories, are they discovering the same underlying structure (the acoustic physics + human auditory system), or are they approximating it from a different direction? The fact that human perceptual categories and geometric cluster hierarchies coincide suggests the embedding has internalized something real about the world — not just correlations in training data.
