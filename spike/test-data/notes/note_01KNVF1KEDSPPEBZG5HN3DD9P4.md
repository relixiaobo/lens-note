The internal/external quality distinction dissolves the cost-quality trade-off. External quality (UI, reliability) follows the normal economic trade-off — users can perceive it, so they can rationally decide what to pay for it. Internal quality (architecture, code structure) does NOT follow that trade-off, because its value is mediated through time: it lowers the cost of future change rather than delivering immediate user-visible value. The standard framing of 'quality vs. cost' conflates these two completely different economic relationships.
