GAAT's formal property specifications — escalation termination, conflict resolution determinism, bounded false quarantine — validated by 10,000 Monte Carlo simulations — represent a qualitatively different approach to agent safety compared to the KB's existing methods (prompt design, logit masking, context isolation). Those are *probabilistic behavioral* controls: they make bad behavior less likely. GAAT's formal properties are *provable guarantees* about the governance system's own behavior: the escalation chain will always terminate, the conflict resolver is deterministic, the false quarantine rate is bounded. This is the distinction between 'the agent probably won't misbehave' and 'the governance system provably won't loop forever or quarantine indiscriminately.' Enterprise adoption of agentic AI may hinge on having guarantees of the second kind.
