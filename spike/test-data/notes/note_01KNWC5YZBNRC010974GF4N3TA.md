The article's two theses together create an interesting structural parallel: AI browsers fail because they are wrappers around a layer (browser engine) they don't own; AI chat answers fail as a web intermediary because they are summaries of a content layer they don't own. The failure mode is the same — an AI layer that depends entirely on upstream sources for its raw material, without any durable claim on that source layer. In the browser case it's Chromium + OpenAI APIs. In the search case it's third-party web content. Neither architecture gives the AI layer real leverage. This is a deeper instantiation of the 'feature not product' thesis (note_01KNVM1WC9B4VKECN2VPE8YVE7): the AI layer is only as stable as the layer it sits on top of.
