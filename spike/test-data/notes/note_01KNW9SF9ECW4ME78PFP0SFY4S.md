The actual trigger criterion for creating a Canvas document is not size, format, or content type — it is *user ownership*. The prompt lists 'content that the user will take ownership of to share or re-use elsewhere' and 'content that might be iterated on by the user' as DO use cases, while 'content that is primarily conversational or dependent on the chat context to be understood' is a DO NOT use case. This is a semantic distinction between two kinds of AI output: (1) artifacts the user will own, carry, and modify — these deserve a separate spatial locus (the canvas); (2) communicative acts that complete in the conversation — these stay in the chat. The canvas is not for 'long outputs'; it's for *externalized artifacts*. The distinction is about the output's intended trajectory after creation, not its structure.
