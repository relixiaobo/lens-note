SQUIRE is a counterexample to a common assumption about AI tools: that 'chat is the universal interface.' The KB already notes that chat is inherently sequential and creates a specific cognitive load profile (note_01KNW5AYYTAAGD1CRF28CKE353). SQUIRE adds another dimension: chat's expressiveness is a liability when the task requires surgical edits. The design space for AI interaction is not just 'chat vs. background agents' — it also includes 'chat vs. structured direct manipulation.' SQUIRE suggests that for artifact-creation tasks (code, UI, documents), direct manipulation with AI-generated alternatives may be a fundamentally superior interaction model: the user retains spatial/structural control while the AI handles generative variance.
