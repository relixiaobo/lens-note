Graham identifies three components of independent-mindedness: fastidiousness about truth, resistance to being told what to think, and curiosity. The personal satisfaction bias in AI research (choosing approaches that feel intellectually rewarding) is a failure of the first component — prioritizing satisfaction over truth.
