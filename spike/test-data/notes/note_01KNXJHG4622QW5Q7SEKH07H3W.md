This paper uses a probabilistic interpretation of embedding distances to derive that, under cluster-wise isotropy, distances simplify to plain Euclidean. This is the 'theoretical framework retroactively justifies the practice' move: the embeddings presumably worked empirically before anyone proved why. The paper's contribution is not the method but the explanation — turning a black-box engineering tool into something you can reason about, extend, and apply to new tasks with confidence. This is a meaningful epistemic distinction: empirical tools that work are useful, but tools with principled foundations are extensible without fear of boundary violations.
