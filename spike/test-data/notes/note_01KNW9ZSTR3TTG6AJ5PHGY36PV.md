Google's framing of the 'Personal' dimension — 'privately, transparently, and fully user-controlled' — represents an attempt to resolve the fundamental tension between personalization depth and user autonomy. But this resolution is architecturally unstable: the more the agent learns and acts on personal context, the harder it becomes for users to actually understand and exercise that control. 'User-controlled' personalization is easy to say and hard to design when the personalization model is built from months of behavioral signals across a dozen services. This is the same gap that note_01KNW85MBB2QSN01WQQDPCAG2T identifies: most users never build the mental model needed to exercise the control they nominally have.
