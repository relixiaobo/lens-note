Arc's founder frames the internet as three user-facing layers: Browser (carrier/medium), Search Engine (pathway), Web Pages (content). This is a user-centric, not a technical, decomposition — and it reveals exactly where AI can intervene at each layer independently. AI on the content layer: dynamic generation and intelligent filtering. AI on the pathway layer: proactive prediction instead of active search (the direction Arc II is taking). AI on the carrier layer: new interaction modalities like voice. The important insight is that these three layers can be disrupted in sequence or in combination, and they have different disruption timelines. Pathway disruption (search → prediction) is already happening; carrier disruption (browser → voice agent) may come next; content disruption is ongoing but slower to restructure existing publishing.
