The three-phase arc of AI startups — (1) traditional software, (2) vertical AI window on current base model, (3) general AI replaces verticals — maps the Bitter Lesson's historical pattern onto startup lifecycles with one crucial addition: phase 2 is explicitly *temporary and foreseeable*. This means the vertical AI window is less like an opportunity and more like a countdown timer. What changes strategically: a startup that knows it's in Phase 2 should be asking not 'how do I deepen my vertical moat?' but 'what assets built during Phase 2 (data, customer relationships, workflow embedding) will survive Phase 3?' This reframes the prisoner's dilemma — survival in Phase 3 requires a different theory of value than survival in Phase 2.
