The 'most AI is a feature, not a product' claim has a direct monetization corollary: if AI is a feature embedded in a product, the correct pricing vessel is the product — not the feature. The three direct monetization strategies (add-on, plan price increase, standalone) are actually a spectrum from 'AI is still a feature' (add-on) to 'AI has become the product' (standalone). The choice between them is therefore not just a pricing decision but a product identity decision: is this AI feature differentiated enough to constitute a product on its own, or does it only add value in the context of something else?
