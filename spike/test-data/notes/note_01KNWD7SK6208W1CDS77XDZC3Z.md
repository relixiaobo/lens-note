Notes are captured without known purpose — this is the root failure mode of all personal knowledge management systems. At the moment of capture, the note's future use is unknown. But any organization system implicitly requires purpose to be assigned at capture time (which folder? which tag? which context?). This creates a structural mismatch: organization demands what capture cannot supply. The consequence is that every organization system will eventually fail for *some* notes — those whose future use didn't fit the taxonomy that existed when they were written. This is not a design flaw fixable by a better system; it is a fundamental property of notes as a medium for capturing thoughts before their purpose is known.
