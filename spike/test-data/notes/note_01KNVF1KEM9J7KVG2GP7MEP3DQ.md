Cruft is structurally inevitable even for elite teams — it is a byproduct of uncertainty, not incompetence. This is a crucial reframe: cruft doesn't signal a failing team, it signals that software is built in conditions of irreducible uncertainty. Requirements evolve mid-build; platforms shift every few years. The best teams distinguish themselves not by producing zero cruft but by aggressively removing it (through refactoring, automated tests, continuous integration) before it compounds. The kitchen analogy is apt — dirtying the workspace is unavoidable; letting it dry and pile up is the failure mode.
