SQUIRE's core design move — replacing open-ended natural language chat with 'explicitly scoped affordances' — is a direct instantiation of the principle already captured in note_01KNWCGCKA9EBJP3MBDCZYXRE4: formalism reduces the model's interpretive degrees of freedom. But SQUIRE pushes this one level further. Pseudocode constrains what the *model* interprets; SQUIRE's explicit scoping constrains what portions of the *artifact* can be mutated. The disambiguation target shifts from the prompt to the side-effects. This is a materially different lever: you're not making the model understand you better — you're guaranteeing that even a misunderstanding can only damage a bounded region.
