The AI-augmented senior engineer should reallocate saved velocity toward quality depth, not feature breadth. This maps exactly onto Fowler's internal-quality argument: if AI accelerates coding speed, the rational move is to spend that freed capacity on internal quality (which has negative long-term cost) rather than shipping more features (which adds to the maintenance burden). AI doesn't change the underlying economics of quality — it changes the *opportunity cost*. More speed means higher opportunity cost of spending it on low-quality features.
