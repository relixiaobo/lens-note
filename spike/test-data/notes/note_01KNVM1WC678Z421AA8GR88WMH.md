Evaluation speed as competitive moat is a different kind of 'intellectual property' claim than code or data. The idea: in an AI latent space, the company that can *find* the optimal solution fastest (via better evaluation loops) wins — regardless of who built the underlying model. This reframes the competitive layer in AI from model capability to search efficiency. It echoes the Bitter Lesson's 'search' primitive being more powerful than 'knowledge' — but applied to the product/business layer rather than the research layer.
