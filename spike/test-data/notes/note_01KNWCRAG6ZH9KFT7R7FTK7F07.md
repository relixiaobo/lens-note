Input addiction is not a habit problem — it is a fear problem. Consuming books, articles, courses, and podcasts compulsively keeps someone in a permanently safe position: you cannot be judged for what you have not yet made. Output — writing, building, publishing, arguing — exposes you to failure, criticism, and the gap between your self-image and your actual capability. The addiction to input is therefore a rational defense mechanism against that exposure. Calling it laziness misdiagnoses it; the correct diagnosis is avoidance of vulnerability.
