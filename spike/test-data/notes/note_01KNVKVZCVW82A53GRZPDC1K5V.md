Personal satisfaction bias systematically distorts AI research investment. Researchers choose human-knowledge approaches partly because they feel intellectually rewarding — 'I contributed my understanding.' This is a psychological mechanism, not a rational one, that consistently leads the field toward approaches that plateau. The Bitter Lesson's real lesson may be less about method and more about how personal fulfillment corrupts long-horizon strategy.
