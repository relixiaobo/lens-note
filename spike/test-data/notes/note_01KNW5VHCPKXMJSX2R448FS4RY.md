Training a model to search via RL-in-a-simulator is a direct instantiation of the Bitter Lesson's deepest claim — learning through exploration beats hand-crafted rules every time. Rather than encoding search heuristics (query reformulation rules, click-depth limits, credibility signals) into the model, you define a reward signal for search outcomes and let the model discover its own strategy. The noteworthy implication: the resulting search policy may use strategies that humans never articulated or would not have designed — emergent search behaviors that only become visible once the RL process is complete.
