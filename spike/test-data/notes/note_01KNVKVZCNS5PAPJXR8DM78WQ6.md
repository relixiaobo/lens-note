The Bitter Lesson names a recurring cognitive trap in AI research: human-knowledge injection feels like progress but is a ceiling, not a foundation. For 70 years, the pattern repeats — researchers embed domain knowledge, gain short-term wins, hit a ceiling, get overtaken by computation-scaling approaches. The bitterness is that the winner is always the approach that felt less intellectually satisfying — search and learning, not understanding.
