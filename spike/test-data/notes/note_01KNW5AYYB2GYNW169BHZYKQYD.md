The three trust-building mechanisms for background agents (proactive help-seeking, process transparency, step-level correction) are not independent features — they address three different temporal phases of the trust problem. Proactive help-seeking is pre-execution (before the agent goes wrong), process transparency is during-execution (observing in real-time or replaying), and step-level correction is post-execution (repairing after the fact). This temporal decomposition suggests that a complete trust architecture for background agents must operate across all three phases, not just pick one.
