Software's relationship to its own building blocks is unlike any physical engineering discipline. In civil or mechanical engineering, material properties are stable across the project lifecycle. In software, languages, libraries, and platforms shift substantially every few years — and customer requirements routinely demand architectural changes mid-build. This means every software project is genuinely novel, and the knowledge needed to design it optimally only becomes available after the system is partially built. This explains why 'we should have built it differently' is the normal outcome, not a failure.
