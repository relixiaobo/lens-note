Software development uniquely resists productivity measurement, which makes the economic case for internal quality structurally hard to defend. Unlike manufacturing (units/hour) or even knowledge work with deliverables, software output ('functionality delivered') has no agreed metric. This creates an asymmetry: the cost of quality investment is visible (developer time this sprint), while its benefit (faster future features) is invisible and counterfactual. This measurement gap is likely the root cause of persistent organizational pressure toward low internal quality.
