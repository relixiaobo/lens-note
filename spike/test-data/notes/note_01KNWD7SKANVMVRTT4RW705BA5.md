There is an underexplored duality between two types of note-takers: those who take notes *as a product* (the note is the artifact) and those who take notes *as a process* (the thinking triggered by organizing is the artifact). The article implies this distinction matters enormously for evaluating tools. For the former, AI assistance at retrieval time is the key value unlock. For the latter, AI assistance at organization time is potentially *harmful* — it shortcuts the struggle that is the point. This suggests that 'better note-taking tools' is not a single design goal: a tool optimized for the process-thinker actively harms the product-oriented user (and vice versa). The insight connects to the input-addiction cluster (note_01KNWCRAG6ZH9KFT7R7FTK7F07): compulsive note capture without thinking about it is another form of infinite input with zero output.
