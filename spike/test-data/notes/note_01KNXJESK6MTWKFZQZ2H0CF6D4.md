The Barry/Mahesh analogy (note_01KNW9BGT0Y9G8TA0B4FYRT87C) argues that situated, pre-loaded domain knowledge beats raw intelligence. LaCy inverts this for the SLM case: what makes an SLM valuable in a cascade is *not* how much situated knowledge it encodes — it's how well it knows which situated knowledge it's missing and defers correctly. The competence being optimized is calibrated ignorance, not encyclopedic breadth. A perfectly calibrated SLM that always delegates unknown tokens would outperform a knowledge-dense SLM that confidently halluculates. This adds a third mode beyond 'generalist smart' and 'specialist knowledgeable': 'calibrated delegator.'
