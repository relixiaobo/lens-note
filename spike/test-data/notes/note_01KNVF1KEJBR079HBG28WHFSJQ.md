High internal quality has a negative cost in the medium-to-long run — making it a rare economic inversion. In most markets, higher quality costs more to produce and price. Software's internal quality inverts this: neglecting it creates 'cruft' that accumulates and compounds, making every future change more expensive. Therefore, investing in internal quality now *reduces* total future expenditure. The argument is not 'quality is worth the premium' but 'the premium doesn't exist — low quality is what costs more.'
