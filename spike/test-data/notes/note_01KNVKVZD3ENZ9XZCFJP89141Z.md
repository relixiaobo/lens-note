The Bitter Lesson and the software quality argument share the same deep structure: short-term wins from a 'fast shortcut' approach systematically mislead decision-makers, and the long-term penalty is invisible until it's expensive. In software, the shortcut is skipping internal quality; in AI research, the shortcut is injecting domain knowledge. Both create a false sense of progress. Both eventually plateau or compound into a liability. The mechanism — invisible future cost, visible present gain — is identical.
